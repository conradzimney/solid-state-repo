Human Centered Design Thinking

Experience

Although I do not have as much professional experience with design, it has always been an area that I have held an interest in and acquired skills for over time. Throughout my acadmeic career at the UW, I learned the importance of, and methodologies used for, Human-Centered Design. The principles learned through these courses and projects are universal skills that aid in all manner of design, business, technical and professional work.

In addition to learning the more philosophical concepts of design, I have acquired important skills for applying these concepts in my work. These include sketching, prototyping, user testing, storyboarding, moodboards, user interface design, and iterative processing. More technically, I have experience with Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, as well as different prototyping technologies such as Invision, Sketch, and Moqups.

Infographic Design and Development

Designed and created an infographic using modern techniques for displaying visual information through appearance, location, time, numbers, relationships, and layout. Utilized different visual models throughout the design process for color, typography, and visual language. Deployed the stages of prototyping and design processes to a wix.com website that can be found here!

Mobile Application Prototype

Worked in a team of 4 to design and prototype an Indoor Navigation Mobile Application. We conducted user interviews, sketching, ideation, paper prototyping, user testing, and iterative prototyping in order to design for our unique user’s needs. Full documentation of the design process can be found in our Process Book.

User Experience Mobile App Prototype

Worked in a team of 2 to design and prototype a Mobile Application for a cruise line company (Carnival Cruises). We conducted user interviews, sketching, ideation, paper prototyping, user testing, and iterative prototyping in order to design for different sets of users that might use our application. We used Invision to create a clickable prototype using our paper prototype’s screens, and conducted user testing using the clickable web prototype. Our clickable paper prototype can be found here.

Echo - iSchool Capstone Design

In order to create a three dimensional virtual reality tool for sound engineers, we needed to conduct a significant amount of research to design the proper tool. Designing a virtual reality application is newly charted territory and does not have the same sort of standards that regular mobile app design processes possess. Full documentation of the design process can be found in our Design Documentation.
